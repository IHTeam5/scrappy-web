So what exactly is composting ?


Composting is the natural process of recycling organic matter, such as leaves and food scraps, into a valuable fertilizer that can enrich soil and plants. With this process, some of the benefits that compost presents include higher crop yield and reduced greenhouse emissions. According to the EPA, roughly 4.1 percent of total organic waste was composted, while the rest went to landfills. The organic waste in landfills generates methane, a greenhouse gas that is the second-largest contributor to the global warming crisis and has eighty times the warming power compared to carbon dioxide per unit of mass.
